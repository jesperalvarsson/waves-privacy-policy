WAVES Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-02

WAVES is a measurement app for Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA), the in-the-moment collection of survey answers. It notifies you with short questionnaires (“beeps”) at chosen times and records your answers.

When you download WAVES you get a standalone measurement tool: you create your own questions and measure something about yourself, and everything stays on your phone. The app sends nothing anywhere.

WAVES can also be connected to a research study. If a researcher or organisation invites you to a study, you can enrol (usually by scanning a QR code), and your answers are then sent to that study’s server. Research participation is optional and is not guaranteed by the app: it depends on there being a study for you to enrol in. The app by itself does not give access to any study.

WAVES is a tool, not a study. The questions you are asked, the purpose they serve, and the server your answers are sent to are all defined by whoever set up the study you join, not by the app itself. WAVES does not decide what data is collected, and it is not specific to health or any other topic. When you join a study, that study shows you its own information and (where required) asks for your consent inside the app before you answer anything.

This policy explains what the WAVES app does with data in each mode of use. Each study that uses WAVES is responsible for its own purposes, lawful basis, server and any ethical approval (see section 3).

2. Scope

This policy covers the WAVES mobile app (iOS and Android). The server a study uses is chosen by that study: this policy describes what the app sends there (section 4), while what that study does with your data is governed by the study’s own information, consent text and privacy notice. This policy does not replace them.

3. Studies define their own data, lawful basis and server

When you join a study, that study controls what you are asked, why, and where your answers are sent: enrolment configures the app with that study’s server address. Different studies collect different things and may use different servers, run by different institutions, and not necessarily by the app provider or by any one organisation. The server is part of the study you choose to join. Whether any study is available for you to join depends on the studies themselves; the app does not provide or guarantee access to studies.

Before you answer anything in a study, WAVES shows you that study’s own information and, where the study requires it, asks for your consent. The lawful basis for processing your answers (for example, your consent under GDPR Art. 6(1)(a) / Art. 9(2)(a), or scientific research in the public interest under Art. 6(1)(e) / Art. 9(2)(j)), the retention period, and any ethical approval are the responsibility of that study and are described in its own information, not here. You can decline, or withdraw later, without giving a reason.

4. What data the WAVES app handles

4.1 When you use WAVES standalone (no study)

This is the mode you get when you download the app. The questions you create and the answers you give are stored only on your phone. The app never transmits them anywhere; there is no account and no server connection. No one else, including the app provider, can see them. The only way this data leaves your phone is if you explicitly export it (for example, sharing an export file); where you send an export is entirely your own choice.

4.2 When you take part in a study

If you enrol in a study, the app sends the following to the study’s server, that is, the server address configured at enrolment by the study you joined:

4.3 Camera

WAVES uses the camera only to scan an enrolment QR code when you join a study. The image is processed on your device to read the code and is never stored or transmitted.

4.4 What WAVES does not do

  • No GPS or precise location.
  • No access to contacts, photos, files, microphone, or call/SMS data.
  • No advertising identifiers, no advertising.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • Your data is not sold or used for anything beyond running the app and delivering your answers to the study you joined (or keeping your standalone data on your device).

5. Lawful basis for the app’s own processing

In standalone use, the app processes your data only on your own device, and the app provider processes no personal data about you. If you join a study, the technical data in section 4.2 is processed because it is necessary to deliver the participation you asked for. The lawful basis for the study’s use of your answers is the study’s responsibility (section 3).

6. Who data is shared with

WAVES contains no advertising and your data is not sold. What is shared depends on how you use the app:

  • Standalone use: nothing is shared with anyone. Data leaves your phone only if you export and send it yourself.
  • The study’s server: while you are enrolled, the data in section 4.2 is sent to the server chosen by that study. Who operates that server, where it is hosted, and how it is secured are described in that study’s own information.
  • The study organiser you enrolled with receives the answers you give in that study.
  • Google Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM): used only while you are enrolled in a study, to send a “new schedule available” signal to your phone. The push message contains no answers; Google receives only the routing token. Google may process this outside the EU/EEA under its standard contractual clauses.

Apple and Google also process basic technical data inherent to running any app on their platforms, under their own privacy policies.

7. Security

  • All data between the app and a study’s server travels over encrypted HTTPS/TLS; the app blocks unencrypted connections.
  • Your enrolment credential is stored in your phone’s secure keystore (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore).
  • Standalone data never leaves your device (section 4.1).
  • Security measures on a study’s server (access control, storage, backups) are the responsibility of that study’s server operator and are described in the study’s own information.

8. How long data is kept

The study you join sets how long its answers are kept and tells you in its own information; enrolment invitations and tokens are removed from the study’s server when participation ends. Standalone data stays on your phone until you delete it or uninstall the app.

9. Your rights

Under the GDPR you can request access to, correction of, erasure of, restriction of, or portability of your personal data, and you can object to processing or withdraw consent. To exercise these for a study’s data, contact that study (its information names the contact); for questions about the app itself, contact the app provider (section 12). You may also complain to the Swedish Authority for Privacy Protection (IMY, imy.se) or your local supervisory authority.

10. Children

WAVES is a general tool not directed at children. Whether a given study enrols minors, and any age limit, is defined by that study.
