WAVES — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-02

WAVES is a general-purpose tool for Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA) — the in-the-moment collection of survey answers. It sends short questionnaires (“beeps”) to your phone at chosen times and records your answers. You can use WAVES in two ways:

  • To take part in a study that a researcher or organisation has set up; or
  • To run your own private self-monitoring — you create your own questions and track something about yourself, with the data kept only on your phone.
WAVES is a tool, not a study. The questions you are asked, and the purpose they serve, are defined by whoever set up the study you join — not by the app itself. WAVES does not decide what data is collected, and it is not specific to health or any other topic. When you join a study, that study shows you its own information and (where required) asks for your consent inside the app before you answer anything.

This policy explains what the WAVES platform (the app and its server) does with data. Each study that uses WAVES is responsible for its own purposes, lawful basis and any ethical approval — see section 3.

2. Scope

This policy covers the WAVES mobile app (iOS and Android) and the WAVES server (waves.carli.nu). It does not replace the separate information, consent text, or privacy notice that an individual study provides to its participants.

3. Studies define their own data and lawful basis

When you join a study, that study controls what you are asked and why. Different studies collect different things; some may ask about health or other sensitive topics, others may not. WAVES treats your answers as content belonging to that study and does not predefine or restrict the subject matter.

Before you answer anything in a study, WAVES shows you that study’s own information and, where the study requires it, asks for your consent. The lawful basis for processing your answers (for example, your consent under GDPR Art. 6(1)(a) / Art. 9(2)(a), or scientific research in the public interest under Art. 6(1)(e) / Art. 9(2)(j)), the retention period, and any ethical approval are the responsibility of that study and are described in its own information — not here. You can decline, or withdraw later, without giving a reason.

4.2 When you self-monitor (no study)

The questions you create and the answers you give are stored only on your phone and are never uploaded. No one else, including the platform operator, can see them.

4.3 Camera

WAVES uses the camera only to scan an enrolment QR code when you join a study. The image is processed on your device to read the code and is never stored or transmitted.

4.4 What WAVES does not do

  • No GPS or precise location.
  • No access to contacts, photos, files, microphone, or call/SMS data.
  • No advertising identifiers, no advertising.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • We do not sell your data or use it for anything beyond running the service and delivering it to the study you joined (or keeping your self-monitoring data on your device).

5. Lawful basis for the platform’s own processing

The platform operator processes the technical data in section 4.1 in order to provide the service you asked to use — i.e. to perform the service and on the basis of its legitimate interest in operating a functioning research tool. The lawful basis for the study’s use of your answers is the study’s responsibility (section 3). Self-monitoring data is processed only on your own device.

6. Who data is shared with

We do not sell data. The platform relies on:

  • Server hosting — the WAVES server runs on infrastructure operated by Karolinska Institutet, Sweden (EU).
  • Cloudflare — network traffic between the app and the server is routed through Cloudflare (a proxy / content-delivery service), which processes connection data such as IP addresses in order to route and protect that traffic.
  • Google Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) — used only to send a “new schedule available” signal to your phone. The push message contains no answers; Google receives only the routing token. Google may process this outside the EU/EEA under its standard contractual clauses.
  • The study organiser you enrolled with receives the answers you give in that study.

Apple and Google also process basic technical data inherent to running any app on their platforms, under their own privacy policies.

7. Security

  • All data between the app and server travels over encrypted HTTPS/TLS.
  • Your enrolment credential is stored in your phone’s secure keystore (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore).
  • Server access is restricted to authorised staff and protected by authentication, rate-limiting and an audit log.

8. How long data is kept

The study you join sets how long its answers are kept and tells you in its own information. The platform deletes enrolment invitations and tokens when participation ends. Self-monitoring data stays on your phone until you delete it or uninstall the app.

9. Your rights

Under the GDPR you can request access to, correction of, erasure of, restriction of, or portability of your personal data, and you can object to processing or withdraw consent. To exercise these for a study’s data, contact that study (its information names the contact); for the platform’s technical data, contact the platform DPO in section 1. You may also complain to the Swedish Authority for Privacy Protection (IMY, imy.se) or your local supervisory authority.

10. Children

WAVES is a general tool not directed at children. Whether a given study enrols minors, and any age limit, is defined by that study.

11. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy we will update the date above and, for material changes, notify active users through the app.
