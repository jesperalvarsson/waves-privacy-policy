WAVES Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-04

WAVES is an app for Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA): it helps you measure everyday life as it happens, by answering short questionnaires (“beeps”) at chosen moments. You can use it for your own self-monitoring, and optionally to take part in research studies.

The short version. WAVES is a self-monitoring app. What you record stays on your phone, and the app sends it nowhere. If you choose to join a research study, your answers are sent to that study’s server. WAVES shows no ads, uses no trackers, and your data is never sold.

1. Who provides WAVES

WAVES is provided by Jesper Alvarsson-Hjort (jesper.alvarsson@gmail.com). In self-monitoring use, the app provider receives no data about you at all.

WAVES is currently in a testing phase. The provider and contact details will be updated when the app moves to production operation.

2. Self-monitoring: your data stays on your phone

When you download WAVES you can start measuring right away, either by creating your own questions or by starting from one of the ready-made designs included in the app. Whichever you choose, everything you record is stored only on your phone. There is no account and no server connection, and the app never transmits your data anywhere. No one else, including the app provider, can see it.

The only way self-monitoring data leaves your phone is if you explicitly export it (for example by sharing an export file). Where you send an export is entirely your own choice.

Optional location tagging. When you build your own measurement you can choose, per question, to tag your answers with your location (GPS). This is off by default: it only happens for questions where you turn it on. When it is on, the app takes a single location reading at the moment you answer that check-in and stores it next to that answer. There is no continuous or background tracking. Because this is self-monitoring, the location stays on your phone with the rest of your data and is never transmitted. See section 6 for exactly how activation and permissions work on Android and iOS.

3. Research studies (optional)

If a researcher or organisation invites you to a study, you can enrol, usually by scanning a QR code. Whether a study is available to you depends on the studies themselves; the app alone does not give access to any study.

Each study defines its own questions, its purpose, and the server your answers are sent to. That server is chosen by the study and is not necessarily run by the app provider or by any single institution. Before you answer anything, WAVES shows you the study’s own information and, where the study requires it, asks for your consent. You can decline, or withdraw later, without giving a reason.

The study organiser is responsible for what its study collects and why, for its lawful basis under the GDPR (for example your consent, Art. 6(1)(a) / 9(2)(a), or scientific research in the public interest, Art. 6(1)(e) / 9(2)(j)), for any ethical approval, and for the server that receives your answers. This policy describes what the app itself does; it does not replace the information, consent text and privacy notice a study gives you.

Where the app provider also operates a server on a study’s behalf, the exact data-protection relationship (controller / processor / joint controllers) is set out in the agreement between them and in that study’s own information.

4. What WAVES never does

  • No location in the background and no continuous tracking. WAVES reads your location only when location tagging is switched on (by you in self-monitoring, or by a study you have joined), and then only a single reading at the moment you answer a check-in. It is off by default (section 6).
  • No access to contacts, photos, files, microphone, or call/SMS data.
  • No advertising and no advertising identifiers.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • Your data is never sold, and it is not used for anything beyond running the app: keeping your self-monitoring data on your phone, and, if you enrol in a study, delivering your answers to that study.

5. Who data is shared with

In self-monitoring use: no one. Data leaves your phone only if you export and send it yourself.

While you are enrolled in a research study:

  • The study’s server and the study organiser receive the data listed in section 3.1. Who operates that server, where it is hosted, and how it is secured are described in that study’s own information.
  • Google Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) is used to send a “new schedule available” signal to your phone. The push message contains no answers; Google receives only the routing token. Google may process this outside the EU/EEA under its standard contractual clauses.

Apple and Google also process basic technical data inherent to running any app on their platforms, under their own privacy policies.

6. How location tagging works, and how you turn it on

Location tagging is always opt-in and off by default. When it is active, WAVES records a single location reading at the moment you answer a check-in and attaches it to that answer. WAVES does not follow you in the background, does not track a route, and does not read your location at any other time. There are two separate ways it can be switched on:

  • Self-monitoring, you decide. When you build your own measurement, each question has a “Tag answers with location (GPS)” switch that you turn on or off. Nothing is shared: the reading stays on your phone with the rest of your self-monitoring data (section 2).
  • Research study: the study decides, and tells you. In a study, whether location is tagged, for which check-ins, and at what precision are part of the study’s configuration, and are described in that study’s own information shown to you before you take part. When the study has enabled it, the reading is sent to the study’s server together with your answer (section 3.1); a study may also choose to reduce the precision of the location before it is stored.

Either way, your phone asks for your permission the first time a location reading is needed, and you can refuse or revoke it at any time in your phone’s settings. If permission is not granted, WAVES simply records no location and everything else keeps working.

7. Security

  • Self-monitoring data never leaves your device (section 2).
  • All data between the app and a study’s server travels over encrypted HTTPS/TLS; the app blocks unencrypted connections.
  • Your enrolment credential is stored in your phone’s secure keystore (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore).
  • Security measures on a study’s server (access control, storage, backups) are the responsibility of that study’s server operator.

8. How long data is kept

Self-monitoring data stays on your phone until you delete it or uninstall the app. For research studies, the study sets how long its answers are kept and tells you in its own information; enrolment invitations and tokens are removed from the study’s server when participation ends.

9. Your rights

Self-monitoring. This data stays on your own device and in your own hands: you can view or export it, and delete it at any time, or remove all of it by uninstalling the app. Because the app provider never receives self-monitoring data, there is no one else to make a request to.

Research studies. Under the GDPR you can request access to, correction of, erasure of, restriction of, or portability of your personal data, and you can object to processing or withdraw consent. For a study’s data, contact that study (its information names the contact); for questions about the app itself, contact the app provider (section 12). You may also complain to the Swedish Authority for Privacy Protection (IMY, imy.se) or your local supervisory authority.

10. Age and children

Self-monitoring. WAVES sets no age limit when you use it for yourself. The ready-made designs included in the app are written to be suitable for any age, as long as the reader understands the questions. If you create your own questions instead, the app does not control what they contain, so they could cover sensitive or adult subjects; questions you write yourself, and whether they are appropriate for whoever answers them, are your own responsibility.

Research studies. Whether a study enrols minors, and any minimum age, is decided by that study and set out in its own information.
